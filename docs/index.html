A documentation project for the กวย (Kuy) language as spoken in Ban Ta Thip village, western Surin province, Thailand.

กวย is an endangered Austroasiatic language with no substantial written tradition. This archive records vocabulary, phrases, and audio from a native speaker, specific to the Ban Ta Thip dialect.

About this project

The กวย people are an indigenous Mon-Khmer group historically known as elephant handlers and traders. Their language is related to but distinct from Khmer, and varies significantly between villages.

All content on this site reflects the Ban Ta Thip dialect of western Surin, verified by a native speaker. Dialect differences mean other villages may use different words or pronunciations for the same concepts.
